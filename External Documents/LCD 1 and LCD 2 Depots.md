
The 333 Woodland Drive depot (often referred to as the Vancouver East depot or Woodland facility) handles a significant chunk of the dense urban core. Because these boundaries are optimized internally by Canada Post for route balancing and can occasionally shift slightly, here is how the delivery footprint for LCD 1 and LCD 2 breaks down conceptually:

### LCD 1 (Letter Carrier Depot 1) – The Core Downtown & West End
LCD 1 generally covers the western and central portions of the downtown peninsula. If you are assigned here, your routes will heavily feature high-rises, commercial towers, and dense residential neighbourhoods.
• Key Areas: The West End, Yaletown, Coal Harbour, and the central Downtown business district (including the major thoroughfares like Georgia, Robson, and Granville).
• Delivery Profile: Expect a massive volume of apartment panels, concierge drop-offs, corporate mailrooms, and high parcel volumes.

### LCD 2 (Letter Carrier Depot 2) – The Historic Core & East Downtown
LCD 2 covers the eastern side of the downtown zone, transitioning from the business core into the historic and industrial-adjacent areas.
• Key Areas: Gastown, Chinatown, the Downtown Eastside (DTES), and parts of Strathcona or the False Creek Flats depending on the exact route splits.
• Delivery Profile: This area features a mix of historic character buildings, single-room occupancy (SRO) hotels, social housing, independent retail storefronts, and light industrial spaces.

### A Few Quick Tips for Woodland Drive:
• The Loading/Staging Setup: Woodland is a bustling depot. Pay close attention during your walk-throughs on how the floor is laid out for drawing your supply areas and sorting your flyers.
• Varying Routines: Because LCD 1 is high-rise heavy, you'll be spending a lot of time indoors working through master keys and buzzers. LCD 2 routes require a high level of situational awareness, street-level walking, and navigating unique delivery points like secure mail slots or community mailrooms.
